Key Formulas

Important formulas used in the NPS simulation and retirement calculations, with brief explanations and examples.

Monthly Interest Rate

i = (1 + r)^(1/12) - 1

Convert annual return r (decimal) to a monthly rate i.

Investment Period (months)

n = (Age_{ret} - Age_{current}) × 12

Total number of monthly contributions over the investment horizon.

Future Value of Monthly Investments (Annuity Due)

FV = P × [ ( (1 + i)^n - 1) / i ] × (1 + i)

P is monthly contribution, i is monthly rate, and n is total months. (Annuity due assumes payments at the start of each period.)

Total Investment & Interest Earned

Total Investment = P × n
Interest Earned = FV - Total Investment

Shows contribution sum and the returns portion of the corpus.

Annuity & Pension Estimate

Annuity (40%) = 0.40 × FV
Annual Pension ≈ Annuity × g

g is assumed annuity rate (e.g., 6%). Monthly pension = Annual Pension / 12.

Return Multiple

Return Multiple = FV / (P × n)

Indicates how many times the invested capital has grown.

These formulas mirror the calculations performed by the simulation (see simulation.html). Use them to verify results or compute values manually.
